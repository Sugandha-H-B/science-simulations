Carbon and Its Compounds

Carbon forms the basis of organic chemistry and can form a variety of compounds with different functional groups. Its ability to form stable bonds with other elements makes it highly versatile.

Format of Nomenclature of Carbon Compounds

Functional Group Prefix/Suffix Example
Halogen (Cl, Br, I) Halo- Chloromethane
Alcohol (-OH) -ol Methanol
Ketone (C=O) -one Acetone
Aldehyde (-CHO) -al Formaldehyde
Alkane (C-C) -ane Ethane
Alkene (C=C) -ene Ethene
Alkyne (C≡C) -yne Ethyne

Saturated and Unsaturated Compounds

Saturated compounds contain only single bonds between carbon atoms. Unsaturated compounds contain double or triple bonds, allowing for greater reactivity.

Type Description Example
Saturated Single bonds only (C-C) Ethane
Unsaturated Contains double bonds (C=C) Ethene
Unsaturated Contains triple bonds (C≡C) Ethyne
