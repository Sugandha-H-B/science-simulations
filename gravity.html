NEWTON'S LAWS OF MOTION

First Law: Law of Inertia

An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Example:
1. Ball rolling on the ground eventually stops because of friction, which acts as an external force. Without friction (e.g., in space), the ball would continue rolling indefinitely.
2. A book placed on a table will remain stationary until someone pushes it (external force). The book resists the change in its state of rest due to inertia.

Second Law: F = ma

The force acting on an object is equal to its mass times its acceleration.

5 kg 2 m/s²

Eg:If you push an empty shopping cart, it accelerates quickly because its mass is small. A heavily loaded cart accelerates slower even with the same amount of force due to its greater mass.

Force (F): 10 N

Formula: F = ma

Third Law: Action and Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Example: A boat moves backward when you jump off it
